Cluj-Napoca

Is a city in northwestern Romania, is the unofficial capital of Transylvania region. Is it home to Universities, vibrant nightlife and landmarks dating back to Saxon and Hungarian era. In the central square, Piata Unirii, is the Gothic-style St. Michael’s Church and the statue of Matthias Corvinus Statue of the 15th century king. Today, the city is one of the most important academic, cultural, industrial and business centres in Romania. Cluj-Napoca held the titles of European Youth Capital in 2015 and European City of Sport in 2018. Cluj (as it’s called for short) is so easy to access and get around.

Sighisoara

Is a city in Mures County, Romania. Located in the historic region of Transylvania. It is a popular tourist destination for its well-preserved walled old town, which is listed by UNESCO as a World Heritage Site.German craftsmen and merchants, the Transylvanian Saxons, were invited here by the King of Hundagy during the 12th century, to settle and defend the frontier. Sighisoara is a fine example of a small, fortified medieval town which played an important strategic and commercial role on the fringes of central Europe for several centuries. The landmark of the city is the Clock Tower, a 64 meter high tower built in 1556. It is presently a Museum of History.

Brasov

Is a city in the Transylvania region of Romania, ringed by the Carpathian Mountains. It’s known for it’s medieval Saxon walls and bastions, the towering Gothic style Black Church and lively cafes. Piata Sfatului (Council Square) in the cobbled old town is surrounded by colorful baroque buildings and is home to the Casa Sfatului, a former town hall turned local history museum.It is the birthplace of the National Anthem of Romania.

Castelul Bran

Situated near Bran and in the immediate vicinity of Brasov, is a national monument and landmark in Romania. The fortress is situated on the border between Transylvania and Wallachia.Bran Castel was built upon 200 foot-high rock, complete with imposing towers and turrets. The Castle is now a museum dedicated to display art and furniture collected by Queen Marie.

Castelul Peles

Is a Neo-Renaissance castle in the Carpathian Mountains, near Sinaia, in Prahova County, Romania. It was constructed for King Carol I, and the inauguration was held in 1883. It is one of the most famous castles in Romania. The Complex is composed of three monuments: Peles Castle, Pelisor Castle and the Foisor Hunting Lodge.The Castle is the propriety of the Royal Family of Romania. Today accommodate the National Museum of Peles, a state museum, being occasionally used for important events of national significance organized by the Royal Family.
